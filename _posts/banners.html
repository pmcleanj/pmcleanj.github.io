Banners

The Lammastide Frontal

St Stephens Anglican Church, Calgary Alberta

The Lammastide frontal was designed by Pamela Mclean and executed by Pamela Mclean and Erin Heibert. It depicts a lush wreath of fruits and summer leaves, representing the "first fruits of the harvest" that characterise the latter half of Pentecost. Green, the liturgical colour for the Pentecost season, forms the base colour, the colour of the text and the dominant colour in the wreath. The text carries on the theme of the fruits by picking out the theme of the "fruits of the Spirit"

The Advent Banner

Emmanuel Lutheran Church, Calgary Alberta

The Advent Banner represents the Holy Spirit moving over the face of waters, while herald trumpets proclaim the Good News -- the good news of the first perfect creation, the good news of God's covenant, the good news of promised and awaited salvation: recapitulating the whole story of God's actions prior to the great gift of Our Saviour. The banner was designed by Pamela Mclean and executed by members of Emmanuel Lutheran Church.

b The Baptism Banner

Emmanuel Lutheran Church, Calgary Alberta

Continuing the theme of the Advent Banner, the Baptism Banner represents the Holy Spirit moving over the waters of Baptism -- and over the elements of the Eucharist. These are the two Great Sacraments -- the sacraments essential to belonging in Christ's Body the Church. The eucharistic promise -- that we are all one body for we all share in the one bread -- is a promise proclaimed to all the Baptised, from the tiny baby in the heirloom christening gown to the adult catechumen awaiting a midnight Easter immersion. The banner was designed by Pamela Mclean for Emmanuel Lutheran Church and is planned for completion in early 2001.

The Adventtide Frontal

The Adventtide frontal was designed by Pamela Mclean. It depicts a planets and comets hurtling through space in response to God's great "Fiat": Let there be light.

The Eastertide Frontal

The Adventtide frontal was designed by Pamela Mclean. It depicts God's simplest illustrations of resurrection: a newly-emerged butterfly taking flight above a field of tulips and crocusses; superimposed on an empty cross.

The Tree of Life Banner

The Tree of Life Banner shows the River of Life flowing from a fountain immediately in front of the Lamb of God who stands on the horizon. Silk-ribbon flowers bloom on either side of the wandering stream, between the towering multi-fruited tree. On the ledge of the fountain sits the Woman at the Well, her water-jar set down while she rests in the presence of her Lord.
